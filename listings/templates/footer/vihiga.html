Vihiga

Geography

Vihiga County is situated in the western part of Kenya. It shares borders with Kakamega, Kisumu, Nandi, Siaya. The county covers an area of 563 km 2 square kilometers and has a diverse geographical landscape, including hills, forests, and rivers.

Population

With a total population of 590,013, Vihiga County has a mix of urban and rural areas. The population density is 1,046/km 2 people per square kilometer.

Administrative Details

The administrative headquarters of Vihiga County is Mbale. The county is divided into 5 sub-counties, including Emuhaya, Vihiga, Sabatia, Hamisi, Luanda. The current county leadership is headed by H.E. Dr Wilber K. Ottichilo.

Economy

Vihiga County's economy is primarily driven by Crop and Livestock, including agriculture, industry, and services. The county is known for Tea and Coffee. Key industries and businesses contributing to the economy include Cottage Industries, Subsistence Farming, Tea Farming, Horticulture, Livestock Farming, Wholesale and Retail Trade, Quarrying and Mining.

Infrastructure

The county has a well-developed infrastructure, including Chavakali-Kapsabet road. Healthcare facilities and educational institutions such as Sabatia Eye Hospital, Kaimosi Friends University , Friends College Kaimosi, Kaimosi TTC among many others are available in the region.

Cultural and Historical Aspects

Vihiga County has a rich cultural heritage influenced by various ethnic groups. Traditional ceremonies and events are an integral part of the county's identity.

Tourism

Tourists visiting Vihiga County can explore its beautiful landscapes, including hills, forests, and rivers. Cultural sites, local cuisine, and various outdoor activities make it an attractive destination.
